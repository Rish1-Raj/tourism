Best Places to Visit in Bihar

Nalanda

Location: Nalanda District, Bihar

Nalanda is a historic town in Bihar, India, famous for its ancient university, Nalanda University. This university was one of the world’s first and most important learning centers, built over 1,500 years ago during the Gupta Empire. It attracted students and teachers from many countries like China, Korea, and Persia, who came to study subjects like Buddhism, medicine, astronomy, and mathematics. For hundreds of years, Nalanda was a place of great knowledge and learning. However, in the 12th century, it was attacked and destroyed by an invader named Bakhtiyar Khilji. Today, the ruins of the university are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and visitors can see the remains of old classrooms, temples, and a huge library. In 2010, a new Nalanda University was built nearby to continue its legacy as a place of knowledge and learning for students from around the world.

Vaishali

Location: Vaishali District, Bihar

Vaishali, located in the Indian state of Bihar, is an ancient city with deep historical and cultural significance. It is considered one of the world's first republics, dating back to the 6th century BCE, when it was the capital of the Vajji Mahajanapada. The region is closely associated with both Buddhism and Jainism. Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, was born here, and Gautama Buddha delivered his last sermon in Vaishali before attaining Mahaparinirvana. The city is home to several important historical and religious sites, including the Ashokan Pillar, erected by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE to commemorate Buddha’s visit. The ruins of the ancient Vishwa Shanti Stupa and the Abhishek Pushkarini (coronation tank) highlight its rich past. The nearby Relic Stupa is believed to enshrine the Buddha’s relics. Vaishali is also famous for Amrapali, a legendary courtesan known for her beauty and intellect, who later became a devoted disciple of Buddha. Today, Vaishali attracts historians, archaeologists, and pilgrims from around the world. Its blend of history, spirituality, and culture makes it a significant heritage site in India.

Patna

Location: Patna, Bihar

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, with a history spanning over 2,500 years. Formerly known as Pataliputra, it was a major center of power and learning during the Maurya and Gupta empires. Great rulers like Chandragupta Maurya and Ashoka governed from here. The city is a significant hub for Buddhism, Sikhism, and Jainism. It houses Takht Sri Patna Sahib, a revered Sikh shrine marking the birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh. Other notable sites include Golghar, Kumhrar (ancient ruins of Pataliputra), and Bihar Museum. Patna is a growing educational and economic center, home to institutions like NIT and AIIMS. Located on the banks of the Ganges, it blends history with modern development, making it a vital city in eastern India.

Bodh Gaya

Location: Gaya, Bihar

Bodh Gaya, located in Bihar, India, is one of the most sacred Buddhist pilgrimage sites. It is the place where Prince Siddhartha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree and became Gautama Buddha over 2,500 years ago. The Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, stands at the very spot of his enlightenment and is a major attraction for devotees and tourists worldwide. The temple complex also houses the sacred Bodhi Tree, a direct descendant of the original tree under which Buddha meditated. Apart from the Mahabodhi Temple, Bodh Gaya is home to several monasteries built by Buddhist communities from countries like Thailand, Japan, and Bhutan. The Great Buddha Statue, a 25-meter-tall sculpture, is another highlight. The city comes alive during Buddha Purnima, attracting thousands of pilgrims. Bodh Gaya is not just a religious site but also a center for meditation and spiritual learning. The International Meditation Centre and institutes like the Dalai Lama's Tibetan Monastery make it a global hub for those seeking peace and wisdom.

Rajgir

Location: Nalanda District, Bihar

Rajgir, located in Bihar, India, is a historically and spiritually significant town surrounded by lush green hills. It was the first capital of the Magadha kingdom and an important center during the time of Lord Buddha and Lord Mahavira. Buddha spent several years meditating and preaching here, and Rajgir is home to key Buddhist sites like Gridhakuta (Vulture’s Peak), where he delivered many sermons. The Japanese-built Vishwa Shanti Stupa atop Ratnagiri Hill is a major attraction. Rajgir is also a sacred place for Jains, as it is associated with Lord Mahavira. The town is famous for its hot springs, believed to have medicinal properties. The ancient Cyclopean Wall and Ajatshatru Fort highlight its rich past. Rajgir's Ropeway, which takes visitors to the Shanti Stupa, offers breathtaking views. With its blend of history, religion, and natural beauty, Rajgir remains a key pilgrimage and tourist destination in Bihar.
